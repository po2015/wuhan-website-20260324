Beam Control:​ Utilizing multi-beam synthesis and radar guidance, the system focuses energy into a highly precise beam (0.5 meters in diameter at 3 km), achieving pinpoint strikes and minimizing collateral damage.
Combat Performance:​ In tests, the system demonstrated an 83% kill rate against simulated drone swarms. It features rapid 360-degree scanning and can integrate with AI and laser weapons to form a layered, cost-effective defense network.
System Integration:​ Radar is highlighted as a critical component, providing target detection, tracking, and guidance for the microwave weapon's precise beam aiming.
Current Limitations & Future Development:​ The article notes existing challenges such as signal loss in urban environments, potential interference with friendly communications, and the system's large size and weight. Long-term concepts envision "microwave drones" and even space-based orbital microwave weapons.
