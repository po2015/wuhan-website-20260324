Use this structure for official company news.

For company-originated news, `description`, `seo_description`, `keypoints`, and the body should use first-person company voice (`we`, `our`) while keeping the tone formal.

```md
---
title: "Concrete News Title"
description: "One-sentence factual summary of the update and why it matters."
kicker: "News"
slug: "news-slug"
url: "/news/news-slug/"
seo_title: "Concrete News Title"
seo_description: "Search-friendly factual summary of the same update."
keywords:
  - "keyword one"
  - "keyword two"
  - "keyword three"
author: "Cyrentis Editorial Team"
author_role: "Corporate Communications"
author_url: ""
categories:
  - "Company News"
tags:
  - "Specific Topic"
  - "Event Type"
image: "/images/news/news-slug-cover.jpg"
image_alt: "Describe the lead image factually."
image_source_name: "Photographer Name"
image_source_url: "https://www.pexels.com/photo/example/"
keypoints:
  - "Key point one."
  - "Key point two."
  - "Key point three."
date: 2026-01-01
lastmod: 2026-01-01
draft: false
---
```

## Recommended Body Shapes

### General company news

- paragraph 1: what happened, when, who, why it matters
- paragraph 2: concrete scope or official detail
- paragraph 3: short close or next step
- voice: first-person company voice

### Exhibition or company event

- paragraph 1: event name, date, location, Cyrentis role
- paragraph 2: what Cyrentis presented or discussed
- paragraph 3: why the participation matters
- voice: first-person company voice

### Policy interpretation

- paragraph 1: policy name, authority, time anchor, core change
- paragraph 2: what the policy means in practical terms
- paragraph 3: measured Cyrentis-relevant implication
- paragraph 4: short close
- voice: first-person company voice when stating company position or relevance

### Holiday greeting

- paragraph 1: specific occasion and official message
- paragraph 2: concise thanks or forward look
- voice: first-person company voice
