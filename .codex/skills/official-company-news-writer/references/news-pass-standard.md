# News PASS Standard

This reference converts [news-reviewer.md](E:/Cyrentis/site/docs/news-reviewer.md) into writer-side rules.

## PASS Gate

To be safely publishable, the draft should satisfy all of the following:

- `overall_score >= 7`
- no critical dimension below `5`
- body word count under `1000`
- all mandatory news elements present at `adequate` or `strong`
- for company-originated news, the summary copy and body use first-person company voice

Critical dimensions:

- `official_tone`
- `factuality_objectivity`
- `time_event_specificity`
- `newsworthiness`
- `clarity_of_main_event`

## Mandatory News Elements

Every draft must explicitly contain:

1. `specific_time_reference`
   Example: `On March 12, 2026`, `During the April 2026 exhibition`, `In December 2025`

2. `specific_event_reference`
   Example: contract signing, exhibition participation, company statement, policy notice, New Year message

3. `clear_subject_actor`
   Example: `Cyrentis`, `Wuhan Cyrentis Technology Co., Ltd.`, a ministry, regulator, organizer, or partner when confirmed

4. `clear_main_message`
   The reader should know the core update after the first paragraph

5. `publication_worthy_reason`
   The article must justify being in a News section rather than a product page or knowledge-base article

## Writing Pattern That Usually Passes

1. Opening paragraph:
   - identify the event
   - identify the date or time anchor
   - identify the actor
   - explain why it matters

2. Supporting paragraph:
   - add the concrete scope, participation, contract area, notice content, or policy context

3. Optional supporting paragraph:
   - add one restrained implication, official position, or next step

4. Close:
   - short, controlled, and relevant

5. Voice:
   - for company-originated news, use `we` and `our`
   - do not keep calling the company `Cyrentis` in third person throughout the body

## Signals That Commonly Trigger Revision

- over `1000` words
- weak first paragraph
- unclear why the post is news
- timeless wording with no specific event anchor
- generic ceremonial language
- product marketing disguised as news
- too many claims with too little fact
- more than one main story competing for attention
- third-person self-reference across a clearly company-authored article

## Signals That Commonly Trigger Rewrite

- no clear date or event
- no real news hook
- highly promotional tone
- unclear who did what
- mostly filler, slogans, or generic official-sounding phrases
- structurally broken or not recognizably news

## Category Notes

### holiday_greeting

- keep it brief
- mention the specific occasion or year
- avoid decorative filler
- use first-person company voice

### policy_interpretation

- name the policy and issuing authority
- distinguish fact from company implication
- stay cautious and concrete

### company_event / exhibition_news

- name the event
- mention date and location when applicable
- state Cyrentis' participation clearly
- use first-person company voice

### milestone_update / official_notice / general_company_news

- lead with the update itself
- keep context tight
- avoid turning the page into a brand story
- use first-person company voice
